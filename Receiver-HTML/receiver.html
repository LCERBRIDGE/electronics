Feed and RF Electronics

The cryostat (cylinder with a white cover) contains the high frequency feed (HFF) and low noise amplifiers which increase the radio frequency (RF) signals of the vertical and horizontal radio waves. The HFF amplifiers work from 2 to 14 GHz. A plate of absorbing material can be rotated in front of the feed to reduce the incoming radiation by 90%. It also adds a known signal, which is 90% of the thermal (blackbody) radiation of the plate, to calibrate the signal.

The rest of the RF electronics is in the receiver box mounted on the surface of the antenna. There is another almost identical set of electronics for a low frequency feed (LFF).

Down Converters

There are four downconverters, each of which converts the two polarizations to frequencies at which the signals can be digitized and detected. Each downconverter can be tuned for a different RF band which is 2000 MHz wide.

Outputs

Each of the two channels of a downconverter has two outputs, one for -1000 to 0 MHz (called the lower sideband -- LSB) and the other for 0 to 1000 MHz (called the upper sideband -- USB) of the 2000 MHz RF band which is down-converted. Each of these sidebands has four outputs:
signal voltage of the 1000 MHz wide band, or some part of that passed by an optional filter,
signal voltage reduced by 50%,
rectified voltage, which is the square of the voltage (negative voltages removed) and averaged,
pulses from a voltage to frequency (VFC) converter.
